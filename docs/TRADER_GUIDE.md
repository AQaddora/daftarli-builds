## Getting Started

### Step 1: Installation

Choose your platform:

- **Android Phone/Tablet**: Download APK, install, done!
- **Windows PC**: Download .exe installer, run, install
- **Mac Computer**: Download .dmg file, drag to Applications
- **iPad/iPhone**: Open in Safari, add to home screen

### Step 2: Initial Setup

When you first open Daftarli:

#### Business Information

1. **Business Name**: Your shop/company name
2. **Owner Name**: Your full name
3. **Contact Info**: Phone number and email
4. **Address**: Business location
5. **Tax ID**: Optional, if registered

#### Currency & Language

1. Choose your primary currency (JOD, USD, EUR, etc.)
2. Select language (Arabic/English)
3. Set date format

#### Account Structure

Daftarli creates a basic account structure for you:
- Assets (Cash, Bank, Inventory)
- Liabilities (Loans, Accounts Payable)
- Equity (Capital, Retained Earnings)
- Revenue (Sales, Services)
- Expenses (Rent, Utilities, Salaries)

You can customize this later.

### Step 3: Add Your First Products

For retail businesses:

1. Go to **Inventory** > **Add Product**
2. Enter product details:
   - Name (e.g., "Coca Cola 2L")
   - Barcode (scan or type)
   - Category (Beverages)
   - Selling Price (1.50)
   - Cost Price (1.00)
   - Stock Quantity (50)
3. Click **Save**
4. Repeat for your top 10-20 products

**Pro Tip**: You can always add more products later. Start with best-sellers.

## Daily Operations

### Making a Sale

#### Quick Sale (Cash Customer)

1. Click **New Sale** (or press `Alt+N`)
2. Add products:
   - Scan barcode, OR
   - Search by name, OR
   - Browse categories
3. Products appear in cart with quantities
4. Adjust quantities if needed
5. Review total
6. Click **Cash Payment**
7. Enter amount received
8. System shows change
9. Print receipt or send digitally

**Time: 30 seconds per sale**

#### Credit Sale (Account Customer)

1. Click **New Sale** (or press `Alt+N`)
2. Select **Customer** from dropdown (or create new)
3. Add products to cart
4. Review total
5. Click **Account Payment**
6. Transaction recorded to customer's account
7. Print receipt

#### Mixed Payment

1. Process sale normally
2. At payment screen, click **Multiple Payments**
3. Add payment methods:
   - Cash: 50.00
   - Card: 30.00
   - Account: 20.00
4. Total must match sale amount
5. Complete transaction

### Recording Expenses

Keep track of all business expenses:

1. Click **New Expense** (or press `Alt+E`)
2. Choose category:
   - Rent
   - Utilities (electricity, water)
   - Salaries
   - Supplies
   - Transportation
   - Other
3. Enter amount
4. Add description/notes
5. Attach receipt photo (optional but recommended)
6. Save

**Why track expenses?**
- Know your true profit
- Tax deductions
- Budget planning
- Identify cost savings

### End of Day Routine

Before closing:

1. **Count Cash Drawer**
   - Compare with system records
   - Investigate any discrepancies

2. **Review Today's Sales**
   - Total revenue
   - Number of transactions
   - Top-selling items

3. **Check Pending Items**
   - Incomplete transactions
   - Returns/refunds needed

4. **Backup Data** (if using cloud sync)
   - Go to Settings > Sync
   - Click "Sync Now"

#### Bulk Import

For large catalogs:

3. Upload file

### Managing Stock

#### Receiving New Stock

When receiving delivery:

1. Find product in inventory
2. Click **Receive Stock**
3. Enter quantity received
4. Enter cost per unit (if changed)
5. Add supplier invoice number
6. Save

System automatically:
- Increases stock quantity
- Updates inventory value
- Records transaction in accounts

#### Stock Adjustments

For damaged, expired, or missing items:

1. Find product
2. Click **Adjust Stock**
3. Choose reason:
   - Damage
   - Expiry
   - Theft
   - Count correction
4. Enter quantity (negative for decrease)
5. Add notes
6. Save

#### Low Stock Alerts

Set minimum quantities:

1. Edit product
2. Set "Minimum Stock" (e.g., 10 units)
3. Save

System alerts you when stock falls below minimum.

## Financial Management

### Understanding Your Accounts

Daftarli uses professional double-entry accounting:

#### Main Account Types

1. **Assets**: What you own
   - Cash
   - Bank accounts
   - Inventory
   - Equipment

2. **Liabilities**: What you owe
   - Loans
   - Supplier debts
   - Customer deposits

3. **Equity**: Your ownership
   - Capital invested
   - Retained profits

4. **Revenue**: Money coming in
   - Sales
   - Services
   - Other income

5. **Expenses**: Money going out
   - Rent
   - Salaries
   - Utilities
   - Supplies

### Recording Transactions

#### Sales Transactions

Automatically recorded when you make a sale:
- Debit: Cash/Bank/Customer Account
- Credit: Sales Revenue
- Debit: Cost of Goods Sold
- Credit: Inventory

#### Expense Transactions

When you record an expense:
- Debit: Expense Account (Rent, Utilities, etc.)
- Credit: Cash/Bank

#### Payment Receipts

When customer pays their account:
- Debit: Cash/Bank
- Credit: Customer Account (Accounts Receivable)

### Capital & Withdrawals

#### Adding Capital

When you invest in the business:

1. **Transactions** > **Capital Deposit**
2. Enter amount
3. Choose destination (Cash/Bank)
4. Add notes
5. Save

#### Owner Withdrawals

When you take money out:

1. **Transactions** > **Owner Withdrawal**
2. Enter amount
3. Choose source (Cash/Bank)
4. Add notes
5. Save

**Important**: Don't confuse business money with personal money!

## Reports & Analytics

### Daily Reports

#### Daily Sales Summary

View at end of each day:
- Total sales
- Number of transactions
- Average transaction value
- Payment method breakdown
- Top-selling products

#### Cash Flow Report

See money movements:
- Cash in (sales, receipts)
- Cash out (expenses, payments)
- Net cash flow
- Current cash balance

### Monthly Reports

#### Profit & Loss Statement

Shows business performance:

**Revenue Section:**
- Sales revenue
- Other income
- **Total Revenue**

**Cost of Goods Sold:**
- Beginning inventory
- Purchases
- Ending inventory
- **Total COGS**

**Gross Profit** = Revenue - COGS

**Operating Expenses:**
- Rent
- Salaries
- Utilities
- Other expenses
- **Total Expenses**

**Net Profit** = Gross Profit - Expenses

#### Sales Analysis

Understand your sales:
- Sales by product
- Sales by category
- Sales by time (hourly, daily, weekly)
- Sales trends
- Best customers

#### Inventory Reports

Track your stock:
- Current stock levels
- Stock value
- Low stock items
- Fast-moving items
- Slow-moving items
- Dead stock (no sales in 90 days)

### Financial Statements

#### Balance Sheet

Snapshot of financial position:

**Assets:**
- Current Assets (Cash, Inventory, Receivables)
- Fixed Assets (Equipment, Furniture)
- Total Assets

**Liabilities:**
- Current Liabilities (Payables, Short-term loans)
- Long-term Liabilities (Long-term loans)
- Total Liabilities

**Equity:**
- Capital
- Retained Earnings
- Total Equity

**Balance**: Assets = Liabilities + Equity

#### Cash Flow Statement

Shows actual cash movements:
- Operating Activities (sales, expenses)
- Investing Activities (equipment purchases)
- Financing Activities (loans, capital)
- Net Cash Change

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Customer Returns

When customer returns a product:

1. **Transactions** > **Return/Refund**
2. Select original transaction
3. Choose products to return
4. Select refund method (cash/account)
5. Process refund

System automatically:
- Returns product to inventory
- Reverses the sale
- Updates accounts

### Scenario 2: Giving Discounts

#### Percentage Discount

1. Add products to cart
2. Click "Discount"
3. Enter percentage (e.g., 10%)
4. Discount applied to total

#### Fixed Amount Discount

1. Add products to cart
2. Click "Discount"
3. Enter fixed amount (e.g., 5.00)
4. Discount applied to total

### Scenario 3: Damaged Goods

When products are damaged:

1. **Inventory** > Find product
2. **Adjust Stock** > Decrease
3. Reason: "Damage"
4. Quantity: Number damaged
5. Save

Creates expense entry automatically.

### Scenario 4: Changing Prices

When supplier increases prices:

1. **Inventory** > Find product
2. Click "Edit"
3. Update:
   - Cost price (what you pay)
   - Selling price (what customer pays)
4. Effective date: Today or future date
5. Save

Old transactions keep old prices. New sales use new prices.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need internet to use Daftarli?**
A: No! Daftarli works completely offline. Internet is only needed for optional cloud backup.

**Q: Can multiple people use Daftarli at the same time?**
A: Yes, you can set up multiple user accounts with different permissions.

**Q: What if I make a mistake?**
A: You can edit or void transactions. All changes are logged for audit trail.

**Q: Can I use Daftarli on multiple devices?**
A: Yes! Install on all your devices and sync data between them.

**Q: Is my data safe?**
A: Yes. Data is stored locally on your device with encryption. Optional cloud backup adds extra safety.

**Q: Can I import from Excel?**
A: Yes, you can import products, customers, and opening balances from Excel/CSV.

**Q: Does it work in my language?**
A: Currently Arabic and English. More languages coming soon.

**Q: What about taxes (VAT/GST)?**
